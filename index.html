Trofee isn't an app problem. It's a liquidity problem.

You're building the Whatnot of MENA – live, social, collectible-first commerce. The product is real. The bet is whether the flywheel spins: enough sellers running enough shows that reliably fill with enough buyers. Here is what I see.

Live, two-sided

02 – Operating thesis

Three laws I'd run the GTM by

01

Supply is the hard side

You recruit sellers; demand follows great shows. So the #1 GTM job is a repeatable machine that finds, scores, recruits and activates high-GMV sellers – not a campaign that chases buyers. Marketplace cold-start theory

02

Liquidity is the product

Trofee isn't "live" until shows reliably fill. Early on I'd guarantee liquidity – seed buyers into first shows so no recruited seller ever streams to an empty room. Protect the supply you paid for. Liquidity-first sequencing

03

Concentrate to ignite

Win one atomic network first – e.g. GCC Pokémon/TCG or sneakers – where supply and demand are dense enough to self-sustain. Get the loop spinning in one niche, then template it. Horizontal too early = thin everywhere. Atomic network → expansion

🎤 Say this"If you only remember one thing: I'd recruit sellers first, guarantee their first shows are full, and do it in ONE niche before going wide. That's the difference between a marketplace that ignites and one that stays lukewarm."

05 – The easy side, done right

Buyer demand: loops first, paid second

Linear paid spend is a leaky bucket for marketplaces. I'd build growth loops where each show creates the demand for the next – then use paid only to pour fuel on what already compounds.

♻️

Seller-led loop · core

seller's IG/TikTok audience → tunes into show → buys → clips shared → pulls new buyers + new sellers

Every recruited seller brings their own crowd. This is the cheapest, highest-trust demand on earth – and it's why supply recruitment IS demand gen.

🎬

Content loop

best auction moments → Reels / TikTok / Shorts → app installs → viewers → more clip-worthy moments

Live commerce is natively viral. A bidding-war clip is free distribution. I'd systematise clipping every show into a content engine.

🤝

Community / referral loop

collector invites collector → niche WhatsApp & Discord groups → drop alerts → group tunes in together

Collectors already cluster in groups. Seed Trofee inside them, reward invites. MENA runs on WhatsApp – I'd meet them there.

🔔

Retention loop

scheduled drops → "your seller is live" push → repeat buy → follow more sellers → more triggers

The right wing of the bowtie. Appointment-viewing + follows turn one purchase into a habit. This is where GMV actually compounds.

PAID, USED SURGICALLY

Demand capture vs demand creation

Capture (high intent, low volume): App Store search, "Pokémon auction / sneaker live UAE", retarget engaged-but-didn't-buy. Creation (build the category): creator partnerships with MENA collectors, TikTok seeded clips, event drops around launches & sneaker releases. Paid amplifies loops – it never replaces them.

🎤 Say this"Here's the insight that ties it together: in a marketplace, recruiting the right seller IS your demand-gen, because they bring their audience. So I don't run supply and demand as two teams – I run one loop." This shows systems thinking, the RevOps brain.
