01 – The read

Trofee isn't an app problem. It's a "who shows up" problem.

You're building the Whatnot of MENA – live, social, collectible-first shopping. The product is real. The bet is whether it gets going: enough sellers running enough shows that reliably fill with enough buyers. Here is what I see.

THE MODEL

Live, two-sided

Livestream marketplace for sneakers, trading cards, sports cards & collectibles. GMV grows on repeat buyers and sellers who stay – not one-time installs.

THE TEAM

You've done this

Farfetch marketplace (8 yrs) + Souq/Amazon. You already know the cold start is supply, not demand. I won't pretend otherwise.

THE TIMING

The window is open

Whatnot → multi-billion in under 5 years. MENA has dense, underserved collector communities already trading on Instagram & WhatsApp.

THE RISK

Thin shows kill

Marketplaces die from empty rooms, not bad UI. Every show with no buyers burns a seller you spent real money to recruit.

02 – Operating thesis

Three laws I'd run the GTM by

01

Supply is the hard side

You recruit sellers; demand follows great shows. So the #1 GTM job is a repeatable machine that finds, scores, recruits and gets high-GMV sellers live – not a campaign that chases buyers. Why marketplaces are hard to start

02

Full shows are the product

Trofee isn't really "live" until shows reliably fill with buyers. Early on I'd bring the buyers myself – pull buyers into the first shows so no seller ever streams to an empty room. Protect the sellers you paid to get. Fill shows before scaling

03

Concentrate to ignite

Win one community first – say GCC Pokémon/cards or sneakers – where there are already enough buyers and sellers. Get it working in one niche, then repeat it. Spread too wide too early and you're thin everywhere. Win one niche, then expand

06 – Getting buyers

Make each show bring the next, then add paid

Paid ads on their own are a leaky bucket. I'd set it up so each show brings in the buyers for the next one – then use paid only to speed up what's already working.

♻️

Sellers bring their own crowd · the main one

seller's IG/TikTok followers → watch the show → buy → clips get shared → new buyers + new sellers

Every seller you recruit brings their own followers. It's the cheapest, most trusted way to get buyers – which is why recruiting the right sellers is most of your buyer growth.

🎬

Clips from shows

best auction moments → Reels / TikTok / Shorts → app installs → more viewers → more good clips

Live selling makes great clips. A bidding-war moment is free advertising. I'd turn clips from every show into a steady content stream.

🤝

Collector groups

collector invites collector → niche WhatsApp & Discord groups → drop alerts → group watches together

Collectors already gather in groups. Get Trofee into them, reward invites. People here live on WhatsApp – that's where I'd be.

🔔

Bringing buyers back

scheduled drops → "your seller is live" alert → buy again → follow more sellers → more alerts

This is where the money really adds up. Scheduled shows and following sellers turn one purchase into a habit.

WHERE PAID ACTUALLY HELPS

Catching demand vs creating it

Catching people already looking: App Store search, "Pokémon auction / sneaker live UAE", re-target people who looked but didn't buy. Creating interest: partner with MENA collectors, seed clips on TikTok, run events around big drops & sneaker releases. Paid speeds up what's working – it never replaces it.
